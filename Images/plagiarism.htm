10 Reasons You Should Not Use Plagiarized Content for Your Blog

In our homes and schools, since childhood, we are told and taught that stealing is a moral crime. Plagiarism is no different. It is as criminal as stealing something tangible. It not only has a moral dilemma, but also represents the character of the narrator.

What is Plagiarism?

If you never heard of the word plagiarism, let’s make you understand what plagiarism actually is? In simple words, plagiarism is the process of copying the ideas or working of someone without giving credit. Generally, online free or paid plagiarism checkers are used to find duplicate content. There could be a lot of reasons of plagiarism.

  • If somebody can’t find ideas.
  • If someone is under pressure to complete work on time.
  • If someone is lazy to think. Yes, you read it right.

Reasons to avoid plagiarism

There could be hundreds of reasons to avoid plagiarism. It is recommended to check plagiarism before you submit your content anywhere. We will discuss some of those which are more important when writing your blog or research paper.

1. No Self-respect

 

There may be some incidents of accidental plagiarism. The author uses thoughts that aren’t his own accidentally in his or her writing. The individual wasn’t consciously and properly trying to see whether or not the research was cited.

When you plagiarize, you portray the job of someone else as your own, which implies that you don’t represent yourself. It is the first reason you should not use plagiarized content because it will ruin your self-respect.

You should think about yourself when writing a paper and consider plagiarizing to see if you respect yourself and the person you are about to take from.

Nobody else will respect you if you cannot respect yourself in your work.

2. Impedes your reputation at college

 

Academic honesty is fundamental to universities and colleges.

Academic integrity implies forceful recognition of all scholarships as a product of one’s efforts and recognition of those from outside intellectual research. Teachers are now more conscious of plagiarism. Every time they receive papers from students, this is the first priority for most of teachers to check if student submitted the original work or not.

If a student is found to be guilty of plagriasm, He/she could face expulsion or other consequences.

3. Bad for SEO

 

Plagiarized content is hated and punished accordingly by search engines.

Search engines sort duplicate material based on several factors. These factors include the published date, essentially curbing the capacity to rank.

So, if you are using copy or plagiarized content for your website, it is pretty pointless. It could cause your website to rank lower than earlier.

4. Harsh Consequences

 

You will face strict legal consequences if you get caught plagiarizing.

It is incredibly difficult to overcome the effects of plagiarism because many trackers will determine if someone else’s work is being stolen.

It can affect your brand so badly you can’t even imagine. No one would like to pay heavy legal penalties or serving sentence.

Quote your work properly and ensure that you acknowledge the author it belongs to, so that you have no future consequences.

5. Kills the Reputation

 

Plagiarism is like a lethal sickness which can entirely abolish your reputation. Many bloggers admit that they have copied content from other sites to use it for their own.

 

“Once a cheater, always a cheater” is a slogan that will act as a reputation for those committing the act of plagiarism. Regardless of the quality of their work, plagiarism testing will be applied to their work, because they can no longer be trusted.

6. Your skills remain unpolished

 

One consequence of plagiarism may be that you cannot know how to do research and how to compose the original content. Such effects can be a long-term threat to you and to future, because skill is one essential element after hard work that plays very important role in success.

You need to write original content in order to polish your writing skills for your blog. Conduct your own research, write your own content and you are free to fly.

7. Fresh Content gets you more Visitors

 

There will be more visitors on your site if you write your blog on your own.

You see, when you are copying, you don’t have exact or complete information for your visitors. If you will write fresh and creative content, you will provide what your visitors need to read. Most of the visitors don’t stay on one page for long time. They just come and look for information they need.

Make your blogs fresh without copying material from other sites. Plagiarism is not a solution to anything at all.  In fact, it throws you into lot of troubles.

8. Harms Your Professional Career

 

Plagiarism can disturb your professional career.

That said, it is dishonest when you plagiarize. It leads people to believe that throughout your career you act dishonestly to improve yourself. It will take almost a lifetime to be considered honest again, but your professional career can be permanently harmed by plagriasm.

9. Everything aside, it is unethical

 

It is really immoral and unethical to steal from someone’s hard work and dedication. You will suffer the consequences if you repeatedly copy content. These may include bringing lawsuits against your website, legal consequences for burglary or loss of trust by your visitors. It just isn’t worth it to face severe punishment.

10. Considering the bigger picture to avoid plagiarism

 

There are many ways to avoid plagiarism for the above stated reasons, such as citing the original references you intend to use. When referencing a source,

  • Be sure that you have done enough research.
  • Make sure you put quotes of that section that you want to quote.
  • Place the source location, so the readers are able to see where you listed the sources.
  • Put down the source writer.
  • Make sure that you insert your reference after you have used a website.

All in all…

Most bloggers and students copy because they are distressed.  They have an enormous deadline and cannot comply with it. This can be prevented by better time management or by asking a superior for a short extension in advance.

Plagiarism will not take you anywhere. Instead, it will get you stuck into a hole of “no more creative” forever.
